# Employee Attrition Analysis (HR Analytics)

## Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing employee attrition using HR analytics techniques and exploratory data analysis (EDA). The goal is to understand the factors influencing employee turnover and identify patterns related to employee resignations.

Employee attrition is a major business concern because high turnover increases recruitment costs, reduces productivity, and impacts organizational performance.

---

# Objective

The main objectives of this project are:

* Analyze employee attrition patterns
* Identify important factors affecting employee turnover
* Perform exploratory data analysis (EDA)
* Visualize relationships between employee features and attrition
* Generate business insights and recommendations
* Build a foundation for future machine learning prediction models

---

# Key Insights

## Age and Attrition

Younger employees showed higher attrition compared to older employees, suggesting higher turnover among early-career professionals.

## Overtime Impact

Employees working overtime had significantly higher attrition, indicating workload and work-life balance may strongly influence employee retention.

## Monthly Income

Employees with lower monthly income showed higher attrition tendencies. However, some high-income employees also left the company, suggesting salary alone does not fully determine retention.

## Department Analysis

Research & Development department showed the highest attrition count, followed by Sales. HR department had the lowest attrition count.
